Betty Marion White Ludden

America's Mother

A time line of Betty White's Life:

  • 1922 - Born in Oak Park, Illinois
  • 1939 - Sang songs with a classmate from The Merry Widow on live Television.
  • 1940 - She volunteered for the American Women's Voluntary Services".
  • 1949 - Finishes university and takes a job in the US Forestry Service.
  • 1949 - She co-host with Al Jarvis on Hollywood on Television.
  • 1951 - She was awarded her First Emmy.
  • 1952 - She started hosting Hollywood on Television by herself.
  • 1954 - Produced, hosted and own her own daiy talk show called the Betty White Show on NBC.
  • 1959 - Made her professional stage debut in a week-long production of the play, Third Best Sport, at the Ephrata Legion Star Playhouse.
  • 1960 - She began a nineteen-year run as hostess and commentator on the annual Rose Parade broadcast on NBC.
  • 1983 - She became the first woman to win a Daytime Emmy Award in the category of Outstanding Game Show Host.
  • 1985 - She scored her second signature role as Rose Nylund on The Golden Girls.
  • 1986 - She won another Emmy Award, for Outstanding Actress in a Comedy Series, for the first season of The Golden Girls.
  • 2010 - Had a career resurgence, hosting SNL, 3 new TV shows and a special 90th Birthday Party Event.
  • 2015 - She received the Lifetime Achievement Daytime Emmy.
  • 2021 - Died at age 99 from natural causes.
    • ""She was great at defying expectation. She managed to grow very old and somehow, not old enough. We'll miss you, Betty." -- Ryan Renolds
